#### [$96 3D‑printed rocket that recalculates its mid‑air trajectory using a $5 sensor](https://github.com/novatic14/MANPADS-System-Launcher-and-Rocket)  
**373 points** · by **ZacnyLos** · [293 comments](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47385935)  

![30‑second overview thumbnail](https://img.youtube.com/vi/zFn__6_LdTc/hqdefault.jpg)  

A proof‑of‑concept prototype of a low‑cost, 3‑D‑printed MANPADS‑style rocket launcher. The rocket uses folding fins and canard stabilization controlled by an ESP32 flight computer and MPU6050 IMU. Sensors (GPS, compass, barometer) on the launcher give orientation and telemetry. The total hardware cost is about **$96**. The repository includes CAD files, firmware, OpenRocket simulations, a bill of materials and a Google‑Drive archive of additional media.  

<details><summary>Interesting Points</summary>

- The rocket’s mid‑flight trajectory is recomputed using a **$5 inertial sensor**, a capability that previously required much pricier military‑grade hardware.  
- All components are 3‑D‑printed and consumer‑grade, showing how consumer electronics can now perform tasks once limited to high‑budget defense projects.  
- The design was simulated in **OpenRocket** and iteratively tested, with a full system overview video (5 min) and a 30‑second teaser.  
</details>

#### [Ask HN: How is AI‑assisted coding going for you professionally?](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47388646)  
**190 points** · by **svara** · [313 comments](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47388646)  

The thread gathers a wide range of experiences from developers at FAANGs and startups. Many report that AI tools (Claude Code, Claude Opus, Codex, etc.) are useful for **small, green‑field tasks** and exploratory work, but **struggle with large, legacy codebases**. Some teams have begun treating AI‑generated code as a “draft” that must be heavily reviewed, while others see productivity gains of 2‑3× on routine scripts.  

<details><summary>Interesting Points</summary>

- A FAANG engineer notes that AI **rarely produces a commit‑ready change** for existing production code.  
- Users report **significant speedups** on new projects, data‑pipeline prototyping, and writing unit‑test scaffolding.  
- Several comments mention **context‑window limits** and the need for careful prompting, planning, and iterative review.  
</details>
